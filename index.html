Traditional Trifle Recipe

Required Ingredients

  • Ladyfingers
  • Frozen Raspberries
  • Raspberry Jello
  • Bird's Custard Powder
  • Sugar
  • Milk
  • Heavy Whipping Cream
  • Walnuts
  • Sherry

Instructions

Day 1
  1. Let frozen raspberries defrost in a bowl for 30-60 minutes and keep the juice
  2. Split ladyfingers in half lengthwise and break into thirds. Place ladyfingers up to the bottom third of the bowl
  3. Pour light sherry over ladyfingers
  4. Mix three dessert spoons of sugar into raspberries and lightly mix as to not destroy the raspberries. Once mixed, spoon raspberries over ladyfingers
  5. Add two cups of boiling water to jello mixture and pour the juice from defrosted raspberry into the mixture, stir. Do not add cold water.
  6. Pour mixture into a glass bowl and gently push down the top layer with a spoon
  7. Cover in plastic wrap and keep in the fridge overnight
Day 2

  1. Stir a small amount of milk into 1/4 cup of custard powder to form a paste
  2. Heat 2 cups of milk in the microwave for 4 minutes and then transfer it into a pot on the stovetop over medium heat
  3. Slowly mix in the paste while stirring the milk in the pot. Stir until thickened and boiling begins, immediately take it off the heat and stir in 3 dessert spoons of sugar
  4. Slowly pour the custard mixture over the trifle, let it sit for 5 minutes, cover in plastic wrap, and store in the fridge overnight
Day 3
  1. Let a new glass bowl chill in the refrigerator for 20 minutes
  2. Whip 1/2 pint of heavy whipping cream until ready
  3. Spoon onto the trifle, place walnuts on top
